# ZDT6 non-uniform distribution analysis

The ZDT6 Pareto front is the curve f₂ = 1 − f₁², but f₁ itself is defined over [0.2808, 1.0] and the search space is mapped through f₁ = 1 − exp(−4 x₁) sin⁶(6π x₁). The resulting *target* density on the true front is intentionally non-uniform: solutions with large f₁ (near 1) are exponentially easier to discover than solutions with small f₁.

- Solutions kept on the front range: **3068 / 3100** = **99.0%** of the 31 × 100 = 3100 final-population points
- Mean f₁ of those solutions: **0.6771** (uniform would give 0.6404)
- Median f₁: **0.6902**
- Skewness: **-0.1871** (negative = bulk on the right)
- Excess kurtosis: **-1.1378**
- KS test vs Uniform[0.2808, 1.0]: **D = 0.0744, p = 3.22e-15**
- Split at f₁ = 0.5: upper half **2327 (75.8%)**, lower half **741 (24.2%)**
